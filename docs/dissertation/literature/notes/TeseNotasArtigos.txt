Explaination of why we choose the Cox Hazards Model as a baseline.
"   My choice of the Cox model as the primary baseline is a deliberate one, designed to test specific,
  well kown limitation of traditional survival analysis. Unlike the average logistic regression model, the Cox model is 
  designed for time-to-event data and, crucially, can properly account for right-censoring. Although 
  the Cox model has positive features it has a primary limitation, the proportional hazards (PH) assumption,
  where it states that the ratio of hazard rates is constant over time, which means the effect of covariates is assumed
  to be the same at the start of the study as is two years later. Both counterparts to the Cox model presented in the hypotheses 2
  RSF and DeepHit overcome that assumption. For the RSF each tree in the forest calculates a non-parametric cumulative
  hazard function, and the ensemble average is robust to non-proportionality. For the DeepHit, its neural network architecture
  is designed to learn flexible and non-linear interactions between covariates and time, directly modeling the probability
  distribution of event times without being constrained by the PH assumption.
